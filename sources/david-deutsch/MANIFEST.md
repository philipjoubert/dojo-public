# David Deutsch — content manifest

Phase 0 complete (2026-04-25). Corpus: 2 books, 16 YouTube, 12 podcasts, 2 articles.

**Voice provenance** — Deutsch never hosts his own show, so every YouTube / podcast entry is `interview-guest` (David is the interviewee) unless explicitly re-tagged here. The TED "A new way to explain explanation" talk and most long-form lectures are effectively solo. The books and both articles are authored by Deutsch (`type: author` is implicit).

**Aeon / Nautilus correction** — the originally-supplied URLs were wrong. The "Why it's good to be wrong" essay lives on **Nautilus**, not Aeon. The "How knowledge grows through criticism and conjecture" piece is Aeon's "How close are we to creating artificial intelligence?" (same content, retitled). The Edge.org URLs for "A new way to explain explanation" don't exist — that was a 2009 TED talk, not an Edge essay (the TED video is in the YouTube corpus).

**Tim Ferriss #662** was older than iTunes lookup's 200-episode limit; `scripts/apple-podcast-transcript.py` now falls back to the show's RSS feed for older episodes.
