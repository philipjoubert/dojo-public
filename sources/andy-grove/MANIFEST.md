## Books

- High Output Management (Andrew S. Grove, 1983; rev. 1995)
  notes: Canonical Grove text. Output of manager = sum of org output under their command + influence. One-on-ones, task-relevant maturity, managerial leverage, paired metrics, OKR origins.

- One-on-One with Andy Grove (Andrew S. Grove, 1987)
  notes: Q&A column collection. Highest density of Grove's reasoning-on-problems voice — "reader asks, Grove answers." Best source for voice samples in coaching/diagnostic mode.

- Only the Paranoid Survive (Andrew S. Grove, 1996)
  notes: Strategic inflection points, the 10x force, the memory-chip exit story. Grove's strategy-leadership text.

- Swimming Across — A Memoir (Andrew S. Grove, 2001)
  notes: Hungary-to-Intel autobiography. Lower craft density but explains the "paranoia" — escaping communism, scientific training. Useful for character/voice ground truth.

- The Intel Trinity (Michael S. Malone, 2014)
  notes: Third-party history of Noyce + Moore + Grove. Context only — quote Grove's words from primary sources, not Malone's paraphrases.
