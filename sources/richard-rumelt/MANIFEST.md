# Richard Rumelt — source manifest

**Domain:** Strategy as problem-solving — diagnosis, the kernel of good strategy (diagnosis / guiding policy / coherent action), the Crux, bad strategy patterns (fluff, failure to face the challenge, mistaking goals for strategy, bad strategic objectives), proximate objectives, chain-link systems, design vs. choice, surfing waves of change.

**Voice-provenance summary:** 2 authored books + active Substack archive (23 essays, authored, ~57K words) + 1 solo LSE lecture (verbatim, 12.5K words) + 4 long-form interview podcasts (~36K words) + 3 McKinsey Quarterly Q&A articles (recovered, ~9.5K words) + 4 supplementary videos + 1 BCG Henderson interview transcript. Books and Substack are the spine; LSE lecture carries the spoken-teaching register; podcasts add modern *Crux*-era voice.

**Corpus total:** ~355K words. ~213K from books, ~57K from Substack, ~72K from articles total, ~36K podcasts, ~34K YouTube.

**Corpus note:** McKinsey.com blocks Firecrawl with Akamai 403. The 2007 article was recovered from the UCLA Anderson PDF mirror; the 2009 and 2022 articles were recovered with Firecrawl `proxy:"stealth"` mode.
