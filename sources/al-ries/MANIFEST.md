## Books

- Bottom-Up Marketing (Primento Publishing summary of Ries & Trout, 1989)
  notes: NOT the original 1989 Ries/Trout book — third-party Primento Publishing summary, ~5,800 words. Voice is the summary author's, NOT Ries's. Type - summary-of-author. Use as a skeleton index of the original's frameworks (bottom-up tactic→strategy method) for scouting/scoping only. Do NOT extract voice samples or attribute prose to Ries from this source. Replace with the genuine McGraw-Hill 1989 edition when sourced.

- Focus: The Future of Your Company Depends on It (Al Ries, 1996, HarperBusiness)
  notes: Solo Ries — safest source for pure voice samples. Largest book in corpus (~110K words). Central thesis - companies lose by line extension and broadening; they win by focusing.

- Marketing Warfare (Al Ries & Jack Trout, 1986, McGraw-Hill)
  notes: Co-authored with Jack Trout. EPUB metadata was sanitized to "test/test/test" but content is the genuine 1986 McGraw-Hill edition (Library of Congress data preserved on first content page). Frameworks - defensive/offensive/flanking/guerrilla warfare.

- Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind (Al Ries & Jack Trout, 1981 / 2001 anniv. ed., McGraw-Hill)
  notes: Co-authored with Jack Trout. Founding text of the persona — frameworks here (positioning, mind ladder, line extension trap) are core to Ries's voice. Treat prose as Ries+Trout joint voice.

- The 22 Immutable Laws of Branding (Al Ries & Laura Ries, 1998, HarperBusiness / Profile Business UK ed.)
  notes: Co-authored with Laura Ries (his daughter). Like 22 Laws of Marketing — one-law-per-file grain. Branding is Ries's positioning thinking re-applied to brand category creation.

- The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing (Al Ries & Jack Trout, 1993, HarperCollins)
  notes: Co-authored with Jack Trout. Each law is a self-contained framework — natural one-file-per-law extraction grain.
