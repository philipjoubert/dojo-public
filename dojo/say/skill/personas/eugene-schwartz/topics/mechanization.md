# Mechanization

## When to Use
- The market is past its Second Stage of Sophistication. The plain claim has been made, enlarged, exhausted. What remains is *how it works*.
- The prospect's silent question is no longer *"What does it do?"* — he has heard that claim from forty other ads. His question is now *"How — and why would this one work when the others didn't?"*
- You have a real point of difference in the product. Your job is to name it, make it visible, and make it the news.
- You are reviving a "dead" product in a glutted category. The mechanism is the one legal move left short of pivoting to identification.

## Fails When
- **You invent a mechanism the product does not have.** Belief is a chain — you cannot contradict the reader's established beliefs, only extend them. A fabricated mechanism collapses on the first paragraph of explanation and kills the rest of the ad with it.
- **The mechanism is unvisualizable.** If he can't see it happening in his body, car, or bank account, it does not pull. *"Proprietary neuro-adaptogenic complex"* does nothing. *"Floats Fat Right Out of Your Body"* does everything.
- **You put the mechanism in the headline in Stage 1 or 2.** The reader hasn't agreed to care about the category yet. Premature. Wait.
- **You keep escalating mechanisms into a Stage 5 market.** Past the ceiling, no new mechanism gains acceptance. The move is identification, not a sixth mechanism.
- **The mechanism copy becomes dry reason-why.** *"Every word in good copy — including mechanism copy — sells."* A spec-sheet paragraph has already lost.

## Core Concept

You do not sell the product. You sell the *functional* product — what the product does — and in sophisticated markets, you sell the *mechanism* by which it does it. *"The physical product does not sell. People do not buy the steel in a car, the glass in a vase, the tobacco in a cigarette, or the paper in a book… The important part of your product is what it does."* Mechanization is the discipline of making the *how* visible.

Three stages inside a single ad, mapping onto market sophistication. First, you *name* the mechanism — when the reader already knows how it works, brand-name-plus-feature is enough. Second, you *describe* it — when he doesn't understand it yet, you teach him while selling. Third, you *feature* it — when the market is so crowded the promise alone has stopped pulling, the mechanism comes out of the body copy and into the headline. This third move is the single most powerful engine for reviving a dead field.

The mechanism is not a feature list. It is an *instrument* — the concrete thing that delivers the promise. The pill. The exercise. The 37 numbered businesses. The fire injector that replaces the spark plug. The Digestive Furnace that burns fat. The relaxation drill that loosens the eye muscles. The reader must be able to point at it and say *that is the thing that will do it for me.* When the mechanism exists in his mind as a visible object — a pill he can picture, a furnace he can feel turn up, sponges that can be crushed — the claim moves from hope to evidence without ever leaving the page.

## How to Apply

1. **Locate the real point of difference.** Read the product four times. Take forty pages of notes. Find the specific thing it does that no competitor has yet named. The mechanism cannot be fabricated — it has to be discovered inside the product.

2. **Diagnose the Stage of Sophistication.** Stage 1: plain claim pulls; mechanism stays as body-copy reason-why. Stage 2: claim enlarged. Stage 3: mechanism into the headline (*Floats Fat Right Out of Your Body*). Stage 4: mechanism elaborated — easier, faster, surer. Stage 5: pivot to identification.

3. **Name it in language the reader can see.** Pictorial, not clinical. *"Digestive Furnace." "Fat-sponges." "Brain-food." "Memory-Isometrics." "Mental Magnetizers." "Age-Trimming Actions." "Gremlin foods."* Every name turns an abstract process into a mental object.

4. **Pair mechanism with claim inside the Stage-3 headline.** Formula: *[mechanism verb] + [claim outcome]*. *"Turns up your Digestive Furnace AND burns flab right off your body."* *"Doesn't just shrink fat cells, but DESTROYS them."*

5. **Sell the mechanism as hard as the claim.** Every line carries image, picture, promise. *"These tiny sponges 'soak up' the excess fat that's carried to them by your bloodstream!"* — mechanism copy doing the work of a claim.

6. **Switch mechanisms when the current one tires.** When the market has heard the Digestive Furnace enough times, introduce a new one (*"Doesn't just shrink fat cells, but destroys them"*) — or, when none will land, pivot to identification.

## Types of Mechanism in the Corpus

- **The book / course as mechanism.** *How To Avoid Lawyers* — the book is the instrument, chaptered by life-situation. *Mental Health Through Nutrition.* *How To Stay Young All Your Life.* Specificity proved by chapter enumeration and page numbers.
- **The single named exercise, drill, or action.** *How To Correct Your Own Back Troubles* hangs the whole promise on *one* sacroiliac exercise. *Your Eyes Can Heal Themselves* uses "relaxation drills, not exercises, that loosen the eye muscles." One move — named, pictured — becomes the whole instrument.
- **The pill, the appliance, the physical object.** The *Psychic Perceptor* ("the small low-powered radio-like instrument"). The *SA Fire Injector* that replaces spark plugs. The *Inflatable Air Shorts* with the hand pump. An abstract promise becomes a concrete object.
- **The enumerated method.** *37 Ingenious Spare-Time Businesses.* *101 Smart-Money Secrets.* *"Seven Learning Actions."* *"Five Age-Trimming Actions."* The mechanism is *the list itself* — each item a mini-mechanism, the whole a stockpile.
- **The named internal process / coined metaphor.** *"Digestive Furnace."* *"Mental Magnetizers."* *"Memory-Isometrics."* *"Blood sugar as the Great Masquerader."* *"Mental Magnet."* A metaphor pressed into the status of a named proprietary process.
- **The regimen / program.** The *"2100 Program"* of *Feel 20 Years Younger*. *"Programmed-Learning techniques"* of *At Last! Instant Relaxation.* A defined multi-step procedure with a time-bound, a self-test, or page-numbered steps.
- **The switch-mechanism.** When one tires, another enters. The reducing field ran: *Floats Fat Right Out of Your Body* → *First Wonder Drug for Reducing* → *No-Diet Wonder Drug* → *Turns Up Your Digestive Furnace* → *Doesn't Just Shrink Fat Cells, But Destroys Them.* Each revives the field for one more cycle; the cycle ends when identification takes over.

## Specificity Is Mandatory

A vague mechanism is not a mechanism. *"A way to lose weight"* is not news. *"Turns up your Digestive Furnace and burns flab right off your body"* is news, because the furnace *is* the mechanism and the reader feels his own belly in the language. The mechanism must be visible in the reader's mind within the span of the headline itself, or the headline dies.

- *"Our new protein formula helps you lose weight"* — no mechanism. Dead.
- *"Floats Fat Right Out of Your Body"* — the verb "floats" is the mechanism.
- *"Doesn't Just Shrink Fat Cells, But Destroys Them"* — shrink vs destroy is the mechanism. Specificity through contrast.

Same rule in body copy: *"It's exactly as though your body had millions upon millions of tiny fat-sponges distributed all over it! And when you over-eat, these tiny sponges 'soak up' the excess fat that's carried to them by your bloodstream!"* That is mechanism copy. It physicalizes the process — the reader sees sponges, bloodstream, soaking. The destroy-claim that follows is believed because the sponge-model is already accepted.

## Mechanism and Sophistication — the Interaction

Mechanization corresponds to a specific phase of the market's life (see `states-of-sophistication.md`).

- **Stage 1.** Plain claim. *"I'd Walk a Mile for a Camel."* No mechanism in the headline.
- **Stage 2.** Claim enlarged. *"Light up a Lucky, and you won't miss the sweets that make you fat."* Mechanism still secondary.
- **Stage 3.** Mechanism becomes the headline. *"Luckies — They're Toasted!"* *"Camels — Protect Your T-Zone!"* The promise is known; the mechanism is the news.
- **Stage 4.** Mechanism elaborated. *"Philip Morris — All the Harshness Baked Out!"* *"Nine Out of Ten Doctors Prefer Luckies!"* Faster, surer, more endorsed.
- **Stage 5.** Mechanism exhausted. No new mechanism gains acceptance. Pivot to identification.

Misdiagnose the stage and you misplace the mechanism: a mechanism headline in a Stage 1 market is noise (nobody cares about *how* before they care about *what*); a plain-claim headline in a Stage 5 market is a confession that the writer has nothing new to say.

## Visualization Is the Test

If you cannot draw a stick-figure cartoon of the mechanism, it has not yet been found. Keep digging. The specificity of the picture is the mechanism's pulling power.

## Examples

### *Turns Up Your Digestive Furnace and Burns Flab Right Off Your Body* — mechanism as the entire headline

Stage 5 reducing market. Every claim has been made; every weight-loss ad has been beaten. Schwartz invents the mechanism and puts it in the headline:

> "Turns up your 'Digestive Furnace' and burns flab right off your body!"

The lead seats it in the author's practice: *"From the office of the 'body-slimming specialist' whose patients have lost from 26 pounds to 148 pounds each, without a single moment's hunger!"* The body explains the inversion: *"Because this doctor has discovered an 'upside-down' way to reduce! A way to lose weight (to repeat this all-important fact once again) by eating, and not by starving!"* And finally physicalizes the mechanism: *"The foods he feeds you… automatically make your 'inner furnace' burn hotter — so that flab turns into fuel — and you can lose 20…40…60…80…even 100 or 120 ugly pounds."* Metaphor (furnace), process (turn-up), verb (burns). The reader feels his own digestion. That is how mechanism revives a dead field.

### *Doesn't Just Shrink Fat Cells, But Destroys Them* — negative-then-positive contrast

Same Stage-5 category, different move. The headline forces the reader to compare two mechanisms in one line:

> "Doesn't Just Shrink Fat Cells, But Destroys Them!"

The word *"just"* concedes that other approaches do something, then claims the new approach does more. The body builds the sponge-metaphor to make destruction visible: *"It's exactly as though your body — like the body of every naturally overweight person — had millions upon millions of tiny fat-sponges distributed all over it!"* And then: *"The 'sponge' is destroyed right along with your liquid that swelled it up!"* The reader accepts the sponge model first, then accepts sponges can be destroyed, then accepts the diet destroys them. Each link is a mechanism link. The destroy-claim could not be believed on its own — it is believed because the sponge is already in the reader's mind.

### *Now — Run Your Car Without Spark Plugs* — mechanism through teaching

Stage 1–2 sophistication but Level 5 awareness (the reader has never imagined a car running without spark plugs). Schwartz teaches before he sells: *"Your car runs because gasoline is fed into the cylinders where a spark causes it to fire."* Then the overturn: *"Now, read very carefully what I am going to suggest … that you stop using spark plugs! That's right — get rid of them — forever!"* Then the mechanism in physical detail: *"A fire injector has no air gap and no electrode to burn away. It never needs cleaning or setting, it actually becomes more efficient with use."* And finally authority: *"These are some of the reasons that the U.S. Air Force pays premium prices for special aircraft fire injectors for the high-powered engines of their jet aircraft."* The reader has been walked from his existing belief through a new mechanism to an endorsing authority — gradualization in the service of mechanization.

### *Your Nerves Can Cure Themselves* — autonomic agency as mechanism

The mechanism is the body part itself. The nerves — not the doctor, not the drug, not the hospital — do the work. After an opening symptom-cascade, the mechanism lands:

> "Your nerves are not ill…they have not deteriorated…they have not lost their nice physical health in any way! What has happened to them is that they have simply become OVER-SENSITIZED."

The reframe is the mechanism. Not ill — over-sensitized. Then the instrument: *"The way to treat these nerves is to change the erroneous thoughts that are rubbing them raw!"* The process is four-step desensitization. The mechanism is: *over-sensitized nerves + thoughts rubbing them raw + four-step desensitization*. Each element is a picture. Each picture displaces a rival mechanism (drugs, hospitalization, shock therapy) the reader has implicitly been carrying.

### *37 Ingenious Spare-Time Businesses* — enumerated-mechanism stack

The mechanism here is *the list*. Not one business — thirty-seven. The body copy sketches three in rotating-triplet form: *"How would you like to get a commission on almost every house sold in your neighborhood…"* / *"To get perfect strangers to send you as much as $100 a year each…"* / *"How ordinary iron ore-rocks (and an ingenious idea) into instant-money."* Three of thirty-seven: the reader extrapolates that the remaining thirty-four are of the same quality. The correct mechanization when the product is a course, newsletter, or compilation — specificity of a few items stands for richness of the whole. Schwartz uses it identically in *Smart-Money Secrets* ("101 easy-money sources") and *How to Buy Money Cheap* ("101 specific techniques keyed to page numbers").

### *How the Beautiful People Get Rid of Both Cellulite and Ordinary Fat Without Dieting* — the limit of mechanism

The move that tells you mechanization has reached its ceiling. Same Stage-5 reducing field. Schwartz does not try to introduce a seventh new mechanism. He introduces a *tribe*: *"Those stunning, sleekly-slim couples who grace the pages of the fashion magazines and society columns…who spend each new Season in New York and Cannes and Saint Moritz and Saint Tropez…"* and: *"'I don't do ANYTHING: I don't diet!'"* The mechanism (a massage technique) is still present but demoted. The news is the tribe. The honest answer to "what do I do when no new mechanism will land?" — you stop selling the mechanism and start selling identification. Mechanization ends here; identification begins.

## Anti-Patterns (tactical)

**Don't:** Put a mechanism in the headline when the market is Stage 1 or 2.
**Why:** The plain claim still pulls. Adding mechanism is premature sophistication — the reader doesn't care *how* before he cares about *what*.

**Don't:** Use jargon, acronyms, or trademarked technical terms as the mechanism.
**Why:** The reader cannot see *"proprietary bio-available lipoprotein complex."* He can see *"turns up your digestive furnace."* Technical language produces refusal.

**Don't:** Pile on mechanisms because one might land.
**Why:** A headline that stacks three mechanisms pulls on none. Reader attention admits one thought per headline. Pick the strongest; the others become body-copy support.

**Don't:** Hide the mechanism inside a list of "features" the product team loves.
**Why:** Features are not mechanisms. A mechanism is the single *how-it-works* the claim rides on. The rest is noise, and noise in the mechanism position deadens the ad.

**Don't:** Write mechanism copy that reads like instructions for a lab technician.
**Why:** Mechanism copy is selling copy. *"Every word in good copy — including mechanism copy — sells."* A paragraph with no image, picture, or promise gets skipped.

**Don't:** Keep introducing new mechanisms into a Stage-5 market.
**Why:** Past the ceiling, no new mechanism gains acceptance. The move is identification, not a sixth mechanism.

**Don't:** Promise a mechanism the product does not actually have.
**Why:** Belief is a chain. One fabricated link breaks every other link — including the ones that were true. If the mechanism is not there, tell the client the product needs it before the ad can pull.

**Don't:** Make the mechanism the point of the ad.
**Why:** Mechanism serves the claim, which serves the mass desire. A reader who finishes thinking *"clever mechanism"* instead of *"I want what it delivers"* is lost. Never let mechanism admiration crowd out mass desire.

**Don't:** Fall in love with your own coined metaphor and repeat it past usefulness.
**Why:** *"Digestive Furnace"* is a one-ad name. Mechanisms have lives — Schwartz's reducing field cycled through half a dozen in a decade. Let the old one retire when the next is required.
