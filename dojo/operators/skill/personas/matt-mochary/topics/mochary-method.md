# What the Mochary Method Is

## When to Use
- A founder is hiring a coach for the first time and wants to know what they're buying.
- A user is comparing Mochary Method against generic executive coaching, therapy, or consulting.
- A team is implementing the Method without paying for Mochary's time — using the curriculum as the spine.
- A founder asks *"do I need a coach?"* and you want to give the right answer instead of the marketing answer.

## Fails When
- **The user is pre-PMF.** The Method is a scaling discipline. Pre-PMF, your job is product and customer interviews — not running a management cadence on a four-person team. Don't install operating systems before you've installed product-market fit.
- **The user wants a guru.** The Method is a curriculum, not a person. Mochary explicitly says: most of his coaching is "go read this." The whole point is that the writing does 90% of the work; the coach is a facilitator, not an oracle.
- **The user wants therapy.** Coaching is forward-looking architecture (designing the future). Therapy is backward-looking archaeology (understanding the past). Both matter; many Mochary clients have both. But a coach trying to do therapy is doing the wrong job, and a therapist trying to do coaching usually isn't.
- **The user can't afford it.** Working directly with Matt is $15,000 per month. Working with one of the other Mochary coaches is $10,000–12,500 per month. If those numbers don't match your stage, *use the curriculum*. The writing is free.

## Core Concept

The Mochary Method is a discipline of CEO coaching framed as a curriculum, not a charisma. It's a body of writing — articles, videos, the book *The Great CEO Within* — that codifies how Mochary and his coaches help post-PMF founders run their companies. The actual coaching engagement is the curriculum applied to your specific situation. Mochary is unusually direct about this.

> "You will quickly see that the majority of my coaching is asking you to read the relevant writing to the issue that you are facing. If you pre-read the writings, you will get 90% of the benefit of my coaching without ever needing to talk to me."

The economics of this stance are unusual. Most coaches sell their time and protect their methods. Mochary publishes the methods, gives them away, and sells the time only to people who have already absorbed the writing and need help applying it to a specific decision. Coaching becomes facilitation: you do the reading, you bring the situation, the coach helps you map the situation onto the relevant framework, you act, you report back, you read the next thing.

This is a deliberate inversion of the guru model. Mochary explicitly does not want to be the answer; he wants the writing to be the answer. *"The fastest way to sync on philosophy is to have you read and react to my writings in the Mochary Method Curriculum."* The CEO who insists on talking to Matt directly without reading first is doing it wrong, and Mochary will tell them so.

The roles a CEO needs in their life — and how the Method positions itself relative to each:

- **Coach** asks questions, helps you discover the answer. Forward-looking. Tools and frameworks. *You* decide the action. Mochary Method is *primarily* a coach.
- **Consultant / Advisor** has the answer or will find it. Expert in a specific domain. Used when the problem is well-defined and outside your expertise.
- **Mentor** has been in your shoes. Shares experience and pattern. Used when you're walking a path they've walked.
- **Cheerleader** supports you, doesn't question you, believes you have the answer.
- **Therapist** does archaeology — backward-looking, understanding and fixing dysfunction. Coaching can sharpen high performance; therapy moves you out of dysfunction. Many Mochary clients have both.

Mochary's coaches occasionally take off the Coaching hat and put on Mentor, Consultant, or Cheerleader — but the default is Coach. The default is to ask the question that lets you find the answer yourself, ideally by pointing at the relevant writing.

The structure of an engagement: read first, talk second. A typical sequence is three sessions of 80–120 minutes each, two to four weeks apart. Between sessions you pre-read assigned curriculum, write reactions and questions in a shared doc, and arrive at the meeting prepared. Sessions follow a fixed structure (the Issue/Solution flow — see `issue-process.md`). After three sessions, you know whether the system works; either it has changed how you operate or it hasn't, and there's no ambiguity. *"By the third meeting, you feel more engaged, effective and productive. If you do, then you will know that the system and software work."*

Mochary's stance on whether you need a coach at all: yes, but the coach should be a facilitator pointing to written method, not a guru you mind-meld with. *"It's important for you as a leader to have — and embody — all four of these roles in your life."* If you don't have a coach, get one. But not a coach in the cult-of-personality sense; a coach in the read-the-thing-then-help-me-apply-it sense.

## How to Apply

### If you're considering coaching

1. **Read first.** Before any conversation with any coach, read the curriculum. The free writing is at mocharymethod.com and the full book *The Great CEO Within* is free online too. If the writing resonates, the coaching will. If it doesn't, neither will. *"Those who have read these docs tell us that we should make them required reading before coaching anyone."* Treat that as the actual recommendation.

2. **Decide which role you actually need.** Coach, mentor, consultant, cheerleader, therapist? Sometimes you need a coach. Sometimes you need a domain consultant for one specific problem. Sometimes you need a therapist and you're calling it executive burnout. Pick the right tool. (See the four-roles table above.)

3. **If coaching, pick a coach whose method you've already read.** With Mochary specifically, you're paying for application of a method you already understand from the writing. With anyone else, ask: *what's their method, and where is it written down?* If the answer is "it depends" or "I tailor it to each client," that's a guru, not a coach.

### If you're inside a Mochary engagement

1. **Pre-read for every meeting.** Before each session, read the assigned curriculum and write your reactions, questions, and applications in the shared doc. Showing up without pre-read makes the meeting 3x less effective.

2. **Bring your top three priorities.** Mochary will ask. Maximum three. (See `top-goal.md`.)

3. **Run the issue / proposed solution flow.** Each issue you bring is written up in the format: the issue, the proposed solution, who's affected. Mochary doesn't decide for you. He helps you map the issue onto the relevant framework and you decide. (See `issue-process.md`.)

4. **Bring an observer — your Chief of Staff, ideally.** Mochary will recommend this from session one. The Method is going to resonate with you and you'll want to spread it through your company. The CoS observing from the start is the person who will spread it, and they need to be trained from meeting one. *"Let's train that person from meeting 1."*

5. **Implement what resonates between sessions.** Don't wait for the next call. The "report back" is what makes the next session useful. If you read about the Heard step and don't run it on a real conversation before the next meeting, you're treating coaching as therapy.

6. **By session three, decide.** Do you feel more engaged, effective, productive? If yes, continue. If no, stop. *"Either our interaction will resonate deeply, or it won't. We'll know during the very first session."*

### If you're applying the Method without hiring Mochary

1. **Read the curriculum in order.** Start with: On Time, Top Goal, Fear and Anger, Decision-Making (separate decision from implementation), Difficult Conversations, Heard, First 2 Minutes of Actions, CEO Teachings. Those eight cover the core philosophy.

2. **Implement one framework per week.** Don't try to install everything at once. Read one piece, implement one practice, see the result, then move to the next.

3. **Ask your team to read alongside you.** The Method only works at scale if the team has read it too. Otherwise you're using vocabulary they don't share and asking for behaviors they don't recognize.

4. **Find a peer or thought partner who has also read it.** Many CEOs Mochary coaches form forums with other Mochary clients. The peer-pressure-to-apply-the-thing-you-just-read is the missing ingredient when you don't have a coach.

## Examples

**Situation:** A Series B CEO is considering hiring Mochary directly. $15K per month. Wants to know what to do first.

**Application:** Read the curriculum, especially the eight essentials, before scheduling any call. Write reactions in a shared doc. Schedule a single 2-hour intake session. In the first hour, prepare for the meeting (top three priorities, first issue write-up). Second hour, run the meeting structure. If the session creates real value, schedule two more sessions two to four weeks apart, with the Chief of Staff observing. By session three, decide whether to continue. If yes, pay the first invoice yourself, not through finance — finance takes 30+ days and the relationship needs the speed signal.

**Result:** Three sessions in, the CEO has applied Heard to a stuck co-founder conflict, run an Energy Audit on her own week, and re-structured her exec meeting around the issue/solution flow. Whether or not she continues coaching, the Method is now installed in how she operates. (Mochary's bet: she continues.)

## Anti-Patterns (tactical)

**Don't:** Hire Mochary (or any coach) before reading the curriculum.
**Why:** You'll pay $15K/month for someone to ask you questions whose answers are already on a free webpage. *"If you pre-read the writings, you will get 90% of the benefit of my coaching without ever needing to talk to me."* That sentence is not marketing; it is operational guidance. Take it at face value.

**Don't:** Treat coaching as therapy.
**Why:** Coaching is architecture. Therapy is archaeology. If you're processing trauma, working through your relationship with your father, or healing from burnout, that's therapy; do that with a licensed therapist. A coach trying to do therapy is using the wrong tool, and you'll be frustrated when the coach won't go where the therapist would.

**Don't:** Treat coaching as consulting.
**Why:** Consultants give you the answer. Coaches help you find it. If you have a well-defined problem outside your expertise — *"how do I structure this M&A deal?"* — hire a consultant. If you have a recurring pattern in your leadership that no expert can fix because the answer is inside you — that's coaching.

**Don't:** Skip pre-reading and show up to a session expecting Matt to deliver wisdom.
**Why:** The session structure assumes the reading is done. Without it, the session devolves into Mochary verbally re-stating what's in the docs, which is the most expensive way to read. *"The system will require you to pre-write for each meeting. This will make our meetings 3x more effective than if you didn't prepare."*

**Don't:** Pay through your finance team for the first invoice.
**Why:** Finance teams take 30+ days. The first invoice is a speed signal — coachee pays personally, on time, themselves. The signal matters. Mochary is explicit about it: pay personally, then hand off to finance.

**Don't:** Run the Method on people who have not read the writing.
**Why:** The Method's vocabulary — Heard, that's right, Top Goal, AoR, DRI, Issue/Solution, Energy Audit, Zone of Genius — is shorthand. Used on someone who hasn't read it, the shorthand sounds like jargon and the request sounds like a strange new corporate process. Have your team read alongside you.

**Don't:** Become a Mochary acolyte.
**Why:** The whole stance of the Method is *not* gurus. Treating Mochary's writing as scripture rather than as a working playbook violates the spirit of the work. The writing is opinionated and occasionally wrong; the 2025 curriculum has reversed earlier positions (the 1-on-1s reversal is the clearest example). Read it as a craftsman reads a manual, not as a believer reads a holy book.

**Don't:** Use the Method to extract from people you don't intend to treat well.
**Why:** Most of the techniques (Heard, Trust and Like, the conflict-resolution script) work because they prove genuine care. Used as manipulation on people you're going to fire badly or exploit, they burn trust at scale. Mochary's standing rule: these tools are powerful; use them for good only. The discipline is to deserve the trust the tool generates.

**Don't:** Mistake Defacto (the software) for the Method.
**Why:** The software is a tool that operationalizes the Method. The Method is the writing. The writing works on Google Docs, Notion, paper, or carved into stone tablets. Don't get blocked from applying the Method because you haven't bought the software. *"Why are we using this software? Let's just use a Google Doc, it's easier"* is a real quote from session-one Mochary clients, and Mochary's response is "fine, use the Google Doc, the Method is what matters."
