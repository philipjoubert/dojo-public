# AEO — AI Engine Optimization

## When to Use
- Your SEO traffic dropped 10–30% in the last year and you can't explain it through a ranking change or algorithm update. Congratulations, you met Google AI Overviews and ChatGPT. You are not alone. This is the distribution collapse.
- G2 reportedly lost 80–90% of their traffic. Company blogs that used to compound are flatlining. Your PMM is asking "are we doing SEO wrong?" You're doing SEO *right*. The thing underneath SEO is moving.
- Buyers in your category have quietly shifted from "google the category" to "ask ChatGPT." When someone prompts "what's the best tool for X," they get a short summary naming 2–3 vendors. You need to be in that summary, not on page 1 of Google.
- You're being asked to "get into AEO" as a strategy and you need to understand what's actually possible vs. what's hype.

## Fails When
- **Pre-PMF.** AEO is a distribution tactic. If your product doesn't retain, showing up in a ChatGPT answer just means users bounce faster.
- **Consumer utility apps with zero research path.** Nobody asks ChatGPT which ride-share to use. If your category doesn't have a research moment, AEO isn't your unlock.
- **You want a guaranteed hack.** The mechanics are still evolving. Anyone selling you "guaranteed ChatGPT rankings" in 2026 is selling snake oil. This is a slow, indirect game — you optimize for the conditions under which LLMs cite you, not for the placement itself.
- **You're cranking out AI-written SEO content to feed the AI.** You're pouring fuel on your own demise. AI writing AI content that AI summarizes is a death spiral — your differentiation drops to zero and the model just picks whichever brand has the most historical citations.

## Core Concept

SEO isn't dead. It's mutating. And the mutation is brutal.

Three things happened at roughly the same time. One, Google launched AI snippets (AI Overviews) that eat 50% of above-the-fold space. Your organic #1 result is now below the fold on a phone, which is where most people are searching. Two, ChatGPT became a *destination*, not a traffic controller. Google, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok — they all added advertising real estate and sent traffic outward. ChatGPT is the first technological development that *took away* marketing advertising space. It uses SEO content without generally providing credit. On the rare occasions it links a source, it links *one*. Three, the category of "general SEO content" got cannibalized — because AI is very good at summarizing common knowledge, and most SEO content is exactly that: widely-known knowledge rewritten for a keyword. AI summarizes it in a better way without giving credit.

So what's AEO? **AI Engine Optimization** — optimizing for visibility in AI chat responses. The best way to get into AEO right now is through SEO — you kill two birds with one stone. The mechanics overlap. LLMs are trained on the open web, and they cite sources they were trained on (or retrieve on). Standard SEO fundamentals — good structured content, authority signals, backlinks, schema — still matter for LLM visibility. What changes is *what kind of content* has a chance.

The content that AI can summarize — generic how-to posts, "what is X" explainers, top-10 listicles — that content is dying. AI is summarizing it. No click, no citation, no traffic. The content that survives is the content AI *can't* generate: real user-driven authenticity, specific case data, expert opinion, unique perspective, UGC. That's why UGC is the only organic SEO moat that survives. No competitor can replicate the content your users create on your platform. Miro's Miroverse, Notion's 20,000 user templates, Figma plugins, Canva template gallery — each template is a landing page the competitor literally cannot clone. That's what ranks, and that's what gets cited in AI answers.

The other shift — focus on **non-branded keywords** and problem-oriented queries. AEO mechanics reward content that shows up when someone asks "best tool for X in 2026" or "how do teams handle Y." Not "your brand name." Your brand-name queries are getting cannibalized anyway by AI summaries. The exploratory discovery prompts are where you want to show up, because that's where the decision gets made. And "watch for new developments" is not a cop-out — this is still very much evolving. The playbook in 2026 will not be the playbook in 2027.

Finally, the strategic honest answer. SEO alone is not going to save you. AEO alone is not going to save you. The distribution collapse hit three channels at once — search, social (outlink clamping), and the rise of customer-as-competitor. The only durable response is shifting budget to trust-based distribution: founder-led social, creator economy, UGC, satellite apps, partnerships. AEO is one thread in that fabric. Treat it that way.

## How to Apply

1. **Audit your current SEO traffic and categorize by content type.** Pull the last 12 months of traffic by URL. Tag each page: (a) generic explainer AI can summarize, (b) user-generated / template / tool, (c) expert/unique POV, (d) brand. The traffic shape on (a) is what's decaying. Stop investing there. Everything left is either stable or growing.

2. **Shift content investment to UGC and expert POV.** If you have a 15-person content team pumping out "what is activation" posts, you're funding a dying channel. Fire half the team (sorry, not sorry), redirect budget to: sponsoring creators whose audience is your ICP; building a UGC program (templates, gallery, user-contributed content); funding a video/podcast producer to put your founder on camera weekly. That's the distribution mix that survives AEO.

3. **Optimize for problem-oriented, non-branded queries.** Don't just write "What is [your brand]." Write for the exploratory prompts your buyer is typing into ChatGPT. "How do B2B companies price AI features." "Best retention strategies for AI-native SaaS." Content that could plausibly get cited as a source in an LLM answer to a real buyer question.

4. **Use AI-visibility tools but don't worship them.** Semrush, Ahrefs, Amplitude's AI Visibility tool — they surface mentions, citation patterns, share-of-voice across AI platforms. Use them for directional signal. Don't turn them into a new vanity metric dashboard that measures "AI mentions" the way you used to measure "keywords ranking in top 3." The point is to understand where you show up, not to build another reporting treadmill.

5. **Kill lead magnets. Build satellite apps.** Your gated PDFs were already dying in SEO. They're invisible to LLMs. Replace them with free interactive micro-tools that demonstrate value — Ahrefs' backlink checker, HubSpot's email signature generator. These become the content LLMs recommend when someone asks "what's a free tool to do X" — and they're a known-good funnel into your core product.

6. **Build in Public — your humans are distribution.** LLMs train on social content. Your founder's LinkedIn posts, your engineers' X threads, your community's Reddit discussions — these are now training inputs. "Growth is now a trust problem" is a line more people have read in my Substack than most company blogs produce in a year. That's where citations come from.

7. **Don't crank out AI-written SEO content.** It's a death spiral. You're feeding the model the low-differentiation content that the model then summarizes without crediting you. Every team in your category is doing it. The output is indistinguishable sludge. The winner is whoever has unique, human, user-driven content.

8. **Set the expectation this is a slow unlock.** UGC takes 1–2 years and thousands of pieces to see meaningful acquisition traffic. AEO mechanics are evolving. Founder-led social compounds over 12–24 months. If leadership wants a 90-day AEO fix, there isn't one. The correct answer is "we are rebuilding our distribution stack for the AI era, here's the 18-month plan."

## Examples

**Situation:** A B2B SaaS has a 15-person content team pumping out SEO blog posts. Traffic has dropped 40% year-over-year. Leadership wants to "lean into AI content" and 3x the output.
**Application:** That's cranking the broken machine harder. Fire half the content team. Redirect budget: one-third to sponsoring creators whose audience is ICP, one-third to hiring a video/podcast producer and putting the founder on camera weekly, one-third to building 2–3 satellite apps that replace the gated PDFs. Pair with a UGC program where customer case studies become interactive landing pages — Canva's playbook.
**Result:** The blog keeps declining; you stop caring. Creator sponsorships drive more qualified pipeline in month 3 than the blog did in its final year. Founder LinkedIn becomes a top-5 acquisition channel by month 9. Satellite apps convert at 5–10x the rate of PDF downloads and get cited in ChatGPT answers by month 12. Eighteen months in, you've rebuilt the top of the funnel and it's defensible in a way the blog never was.

**Situation:** A dev-tools company notices that prospects in sales calls reference "ChatGPT told me to check out [competitor]" and not them. CEO asks how to "rank in ChatGPT."
**Application:** You don't rank in ChatGPT — you *get cited* by ChatGPT, and only indirectly. The mechanics: you want to be the expert source the model retrieves. That means founder-led thought leadership (CEO on LinkedIn, substack, podcasts), unique expert content on the specific problems buyers ask about, and crucially — *getting mentioned by other trusted voices.* When Lenny, MK1, or a competitor's blog links to you, those links become training signal. Sponsor 5–10 creators in the dev-tools niche. Run a "here's how to pick between X and Y" comparison landing page written by the CTO, not a content marketer. Build a free satellite tool that solves a buyer's real workflow problem.
**Result:** By month 9, you start seeing your brand show up in LLM answers to exploratory prompts. You notice because HDYHAU responses shift — more users say "ChatGPT mentioned you" or "saw you in a comparison." Sales calls stop opening with "why not [competitor]" and start opening with specific questions that came from a thoughtful comparison page you wrote. That's the real win.

**Situation:** A marketing lead is being asked to "prove AEO ROI" 60 days into a program.
**Application:** Refuse the premise. You cannot prove AEO ROI in 60 days because LLM citations are retrieved from content that was created and indexed months earlier. What you *can* do: track leading indicators — brand mentions in AI responses (via tools like Semrush One/Ahrefs Brand Radar), share-of-voice against competitors, HDYHAU responses citing AI tools, satellite app traffic, founder social reach. Build a ramp chart. Commit to evaluating at 6, 12, and 18 months against those leading indicators.
**Result:** Leadership gets a clear mental model: AEO is a trust-based distribution play with a slow compound curve, same mechanics as UGC and founder-led social, and the right time to evaluate is annually. The quarterly pressure to "prove ROI" gets redirected to the tactics that deserve it (paid acquisition, product-led conversion), and AEO gets the time horizon it actually needs.

## Anti-Patterns (tactical)

**Don't:** Crank out AI-generated SEO content to "feed the models."
**Why:** Low-differentiation content is exactly what the model summarizes without crediting you. You're pouring budget into content that makes the model better while erasing your distinctiveness. Every competitor is doing it. The signal-to-noise ratio collapses. The only way to stand out is content AI *can't* generate — UGC, expert POV, unique data.

**Don't:** Optimize for branded queries as your AEO strategy.
**Why:** Branded queries are the easiest for AI summaries to cannibalize, and they mostly serve users who already found you. Non-branded, problem-oriented queries are where discovery happens. That's where AEO has leverage.

**Don't:** Trust "AEO agencies" selling guaranteed rankings.
**Why:** Nobody has a reliable lever over LLM citations yet. The mechanics are evolving quarterly. Agencies pitching guaranteed placement are either overstating what they can do or running black-hat tactics that will get you penalized when the platforms tighten. Bet on fundamentals: good content, trusted mentions, expert POV, UGC.

**Don't:** Throw away traditional SEO entirely.
**Why:** SEO and AEO overlap. The best way into AEO right now is through SEO fundamentals — authority, structure, backlinks. Abandoning SEO loses both channels. Shift investment toward the content types that survive (UGC, expert POV), don't delete the whole stack.

**Don't:** Set up "AI mentions" as a new vanity dashboard.
**Why:** Same trap as keyword-ranking dashboards from 2015 — a metric leadership chases that doesn't correlate with revenue. AI mentions are a directional signal. HDYHAU answers, activation rate, pipeline attribution — those are the metrics that matter. Use AI-visibility tools to inform the strategy, not to invent a new KPI factory.

**Don't:** Expect AEO to replace the need for creator partnerships and founder social.
**Why:** AEO is one thread in post-SEO distribution. The compounding surface is trust — founder-led social, creator economy, UGC, satellite apps. AEO rides on top of those. If you rebuild only AEO and ignore the rest, you've optimized 10% of the solution to a 100% problem.
